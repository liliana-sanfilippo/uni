\subsection*{Traditional NED: Individual Approaches}
\zitat{Independent approaches use semantic similarity techniques to rank candidate entities
solely according to their lexical similarity and/or empirical co-occurrence with mentions.
In these methods, each mention is independently disambiguated, and they consider
entity disambiguation as a ranking problem that picks the entity with the highest
confidence.}{Al-Moslmi et al. 2020}

\subsection*{Traditional NED: Collective Approaches}
\zitat{Collective approaches take into account that what is mentioned in the same text tends
to be about the same topic and that co-occurring entities should therefore often be
semantically related. Most commonly used collective approaches establish associations
between candidate entities and build mention-entity pairs using a probabilistic graph
approach.}{Al-Moslmi et al. 2020}

\begin{bsp}
    GDISTIS (A novel multilingual, knowledge-base agnostic and
    deterministic approach to entity linking), Babelfy (unified graph-based approach to
    multilingual Entity Linking and Word Sense Disambiguation), TagME use ``graph
    connectedness to exploit semantic relations''.
\end{bsp}

\subsection*{Neural Network-Based NED}
\zitat{In recent years, neural-network (NN) based methods have become more common and
achieved competitive results. One family of approaches map words into continuous
vector spaces using word2vec. Another family uses deep NNs to learn the latent
semantic features automatically. NED accuracy is thereby enhanced along with the
model’s generalization ability.}{Al-Moslmi et al. 2020}

More recently, attention mechanisms have been introduced to assign graded
importance... a multi-perspective attention NN model was proposed to enrich mentions
and entities representation.

Empirical evaluations show that NN-based approaches are effective in NED systems.

\subsection*{Joint NER and NED}
Motivation: Many previous studies have dealt with NER and NED in two separate steps. The
consequence is that NED may not use all the information provided by NER. Named
Entity Recognition and Disambiguation (NERD) therefore deals with NER and NED
jointly.

\begin{bsp}
    \textbf{Example Methods}
    \begin{itemize}
        \item ERL model: utilizes the mutual dependency between NER and NED
        \item J-NERD: uses a probabilistic graph model
        \item TwitterNEED: supports NERD of short and informal tweets
    \end{itemize}
\end{bsp}
